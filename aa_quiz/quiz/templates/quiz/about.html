The Art Advisory Quiz

A lead-generation tool for an online art gallery, with customer persona identification logic and 'likelihood-to-convert' metrics.

Background:

I work at an online art gallery as an art advisor, and one of our main lead-generation tools is an art advisory questionnaire where users can request personalized artwork suggestions from an art advisor. As an art advisor, I respond to these requests by hand-curating a selection of artworks that I think the user will like, based on the information in their quiz.


Problem:

Our current quiz experience does not provide the art advisor with any information about the lead in terms of their likelihood to convert, nor does it identify a lead by what customer persona bucket they fall into. As the business scales and our lead numbers grow, art advisors don't know how to prioritize new leads, and a high intent request might wait 5+ days for a response, growing cold and/or frustrated.


Proposal:

This project addresses the above issues by creating a new quiz flow that will capture both intent and customer persona type of the user based on their responses. This data will allow advisors to address high intent leads first, users can be guided to content that is relevant to them while they wait, and low intent leads can be weeded out.


Product Notes:

Rather than building a public-facing quiz that is ready for leads to use, I've built an admin-transparent quiz that demonstrates:

  1. data collection and scoring as users answer quiz questions;
  2. an end result categorization, based on analysis of the users responses;
  3. suggestions for relevant content to be served to the user, based on their identified persona type.

When you take the quiz, you will notice a score being awarded as you answer questions. Keep in mind that these scores:

  1. have arbitrary values, but are meant to show that certain types of inquiries should receive higher value than others;
  2. would benefit from recalibration after an analysis of users' predicted intent versus actual purchase behaviours;
  3. do not have an upper limit, but a cap could be determined after some user testing.
